Fiddle Leaf Fig

About Fiddle Leaf Fig

The fiddle leaf fig (Ficus lyrata) is a popular houseplant known for its large, glossy, violin-shaped leaves. Native to tropical regions of West Africa, this plant is admired for its dramatic appearance and ability to add a touch of elegance to indoor spaces.

Care Tips

  • Light: Requires bright, indirect light. Avoid direct sunlight, which can scorch the leaves.
  • Water: Water when the top inch of soil feels dry. Overwatering can cause root rot.
  • Humidity: Prefers high humidity levels. Consider misting the leaves or using a humidifier.
  • Temperature: Thrives in temperatures between 60-75°F (16-24°C).
  • Soil: Use well-draining soil to prevent waterlogging.

Benefits

Fiddle leaf figs are excellent for improving indoor air quality by filtering out toxins. Their large leaves also add a bold visual statement to home decor, making them a favorite among interior designers.

Pruning and Maintenance

To maintain its shape and promote healthy growth, prune the fiddle leaf fig by trimming off any damaged or yellowing leaves. Rotate the plant occasionally to ensure even growth on all sides.

Fun Facts

  • The fiddle leaf fig gets its name from the unique shape of its leaves, which resemble a violin or fiddle.
  • It can grow up to 10 feet tall indoors under optimal conditions.
