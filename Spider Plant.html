Spider Plant

About Spider Plant

The spider plant, scientifically known as Chlorophytum comosum, is a fast-growing and easy-to-care-for houseplant. It is loved for its unique arching leaves and tiny spiderettes that dangle from the mother plant like a web.

Care Tips

  • Light: Prefers bright, indirect sunlight but tolerates low light conditions.
  • Water: Keep the soil slightly moist but not waterlogged. Allow the top inch of soil to dry out before watering.
  • Temperature: Thrives in temperatures between 65-75°F (18-24°C).
  • Humidity: Appreciates moderate to high humidity.

Benefits

Spider plants are excellent air purifiers and can remove toxins such as carbon monoxide, formaldehyde, and benzene. They are non-toxic to pets and humans, making them a safe choice for households with animals or children.

Propagation

Spider plants are one of the easiest houseplants to propagate. Simply cut off the spiderettes and place them in water or soil until roots develop.
